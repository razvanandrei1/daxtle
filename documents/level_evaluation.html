13Crossing Paths

8 / 10
Difficulty
6 / 10
CrossPush chains2 ColorsCrossing paths
14 cells · 4 blocks (2+2) · 2 colors

Elegant redesign. Distilled from 8 blocks on 24 cells to 4 blocks on 14 — every cell earns its place. The cross shape with missing corners is distinctive. 6-move solution (right, up×3, right, up) has a brilliant ordering trap: B2 must reach [2,1] early (move 4) to act as a wall preventing B1 from escaping right, then B1@[1,0] vacates to [2,0] (move 5), and the final push slides B1 into the now-empty [1,0]. Push chains, cross-color blocking, and a non-obvious right-up-right alternation. Major upgrade over the old 6×6 army version.

14Sacrifice
Quality
8 / 10
Difficulty
6 / 10
TutorialDiamondDestroy blockPush chain2 Colors5 blocks
12 cells · 5 blocks · 2 colors · 1 destroy block

Excellent D-block introduction. Cutting all four corners creates a diamond silhouette — compact and distinctive. 5 blocks on 12 cells is dense; every cell earns its place. 9-move solution in three clean phases: (1) column 2 sacrifice — down pushes left into the D cell, then walks to [2,3]; (2) B2 crosses the board left×3 to [0,1]; (3) column 1 push chain — right positions, then up pushes it through [1,1] and [1,0]. Critical ordering trap: B2 must cross column 1 before the push chain fills [1,1]. Shape and puzzle logic now both deliver.

"Push a block onto the X to destroy it."

8 / 10
Difficulty
6 / 10
HourglassDestroy blockColumn contestCross-color routing2 Colors5 blocks
16 cells · 5 blocks · 2 colors · 1 destroy block

Second color elevates everything. Tightened to 16 cells with a distinctive hourglass silhouette — single cell top, widening to 6-cell row 3, narrowing to 2 at bottom. Three interacting systems: (1) column 2 vertical contest — B1[2,0]▼ and B2[2,4]▲ compete for the same column, but B2 (red) needs T2 at [3,2], requiring a push to change columns; (2) row 3 sequencing gate — [5,3]◀ blocked by [3,3]◀, forcing ordered resolution toward [0,3] and [1,3]; (3) destroy sacrifice at [1,2] adjacent to targets. The cross-color push routing creates a genuine "aha" moment — B2 can't reach its target alone. Every cell earns its place at 0.31 density.
